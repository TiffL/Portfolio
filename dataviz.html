Game Sales Dashboard

Interactive data visualization dashboard displaying and exploring video game sales across genres, publishers, and years. Video games sales dataset is from Kaggle.

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • JavaScript
  • D3.js

Context

The goal for this project was to design a dashboard that effectively visualizes video game sales data to meet the needs of a potential audience looking to perform exploratory analysis. In order to be effective, it needed to accomplish the following: have an easily understandable and navigatable interface, clearly communicate potential trends or patterns (or lack therof) to follow up on, and efficiently display the large amount of data and information in a smaller and more compact visual space.

Audience

Although the data is of interest to many different audiences, this dashboard views the data from the perspective of a game developer looking to create a new product. The intended audience, then, may be looking to answer the following questions as part of their exploratory analysis:

  • Which games have had the most sales? This could be a gateway into further research on why these top-selling games peformed so well.
  • Who are the competitors in their intended genre? Who are the major players?
  • How popular is their intended genre in a particular region?


Dataset

The Kaggle dataset contains the following fields: video game name, ranking, year published, genre, publisher, and sales. Sales data includes both total global sales and sales broken up into regional categories - North America, Europe, Japan, and Other. This isn't ideal, since the regional groupings are of differing levels of specificity (continents vs. countries vs. everywhere else), making it difficult to do comparisons between regions. Due to time constraints, I used the categories as is for this project, but an extension of this project could benefit from sourcing additional data from other places.



Design & Graphs

The next step was to put together the questions and the dataset. Each section of the dashboard addresses one of the questions above.


Section 1: Which games have had the most sales?

This section shows the top 10 best-selling video games of all time, as well as for each individual release year. Rank is determined by the video game's total global sales.

Section 2: Who are the competitors in their intended genre? Who are the major players?

This section consists of two views: a rankings and a stats view. The rankings view displays the 20 best selling publishers for each genre, providing insight as to who the major players are in each.

It is important to keep in mind that these major players tend to be extreme outliers in terms of total sales when compared to the average total sales in their genre, however. As a result, developers may prefer to use the stats view instead to get a better sense of what the average company can expect to earn in terms of global sales and to identify similarly positioned developers that are more likely to be competitors.

Stats view gives an overview of publishers in each genre and where they stand in relation to one another in terms of total sales within the genre.

Both individual data points and box plots are shown. Here the focus is on the box plots. The lower right toggle, however, gives the option of focusing on the points instead. Jitter is included to limit overlaps and to give a better sense of the data's distribution.

Tooltips are also included to provide a more detailed look at the data. Hovering over a point reveals the publisher's name and sales, while hovering over a plot brings up statistics for the genre's publisher total sales.

Section 3: How popular is their intended genre in a particular region?

For this section, total sales across all publishers is aggregated for each region in the dataset - North America, Europe, Japan, and "Other". The map provides a quick overview of results, displaying the best-selling genre for each region.

Several GeoJSON objects (one for each of the dataset's regions) were combined to provide interactivity for additional detail. Hovering over each region updates a pie chart breaking down the region's genre sales, allowing developers to gauge their intended genre's relative share of the region's market.
