ePubbly

ePubbly is a browser-based ePub viewer. It was built with the aim of giving people (especially Windows users) the option of viewing their .epub files online without having to download additional software.

Context

Earlier in May, a friend was looking for recommendations for online ePub readers, since Microsoft Edge had stopped their support for .epub. A quick online search revealed that although there are existing options for ePub readers, most are either in the form of browser extensions or downloadable software. This isn't ideal for several groups of users, including those looking to read their .epubs accross multiple devices (as it would require redownloading a copy of the extension or software for each device), and those with limited device space. Of the few in-browser options found, none seemed to offer the customizability and assessibility (dark mode, larger font) of existing downloadable options.

As a result, I decided to build one. My goal for this project, then, was to create a web app that allows users to view their .epub files in the browser, with the process as streamlined and simple as possible.

Tools

  • Vue.js

Design


In keeping with the site's "no hassle" tagline, I went with a minimalistic look and feel for the site. Keeping both the navigation and appearance simple will help minimize distractions, hopefully improving the experience for most users whose goal is to focus on their book or document.

To achieve a relaxed feel, the color palette consists of more muted colors. The project's title is ePubbly (think "bubbly"), though, so to keep the mood rather joyful as well, I went with colors on the lighter end of the spectrum - a warm beige primary color with a brighter blue accent.


Users are greeted by a landing page with a single button to choose a file. Only .epub files are accepted, and the file navigator greys out non-epub files, making it easier for users to locate the files they need.

The reading view keeps tools accessible at the top of the screen, but with decreased contrast to avoid competing with the document for the user's attention.

These tools include a clickable table of contents for quick navigation through the file, as well as customizability options.

One of these options is dark mode, for a better nighttime reading experience.
